## The Problem We Solve

### Industry Context
The U.S. trucking industry moves **$800+ billion** in freight annually. Yet it operates with:
- No unified identity system
- No way to verify who actually has your load
- Documents scattered across texts, emails, and cab folders
- Rampant fraud and double brokering

### Core Problems (Use These in Problem Section)

#### 1. Double Brokering Epidemic
**What it is:** Carriers illegally re-broker loads to unknown third parties without the original broker's knowledge.

**The impact:**
- $500M+ lost annually to double brokering
- Brokers have no idea who actually has their freight
- When something goes wrong, there's no accountability
- Cargo theft often follows double brokering

**Quote-worthy stat:** "67% increase in cargo theft in 2023, often linked to double brokering schemes"

#### 2. Identity Fraud & Fake Carriers
**What it is:** Criminals create fake carrier identities using spoofed MC numbers, stolen credentials, and burner phones.

**The impact:**
- Brokers can't verify who they're actually dealing with
- Fake carriers accept loads, then steal the freight
- No way to know if the driver who shows up is the assigned driver
- Phone numbers and emails are easily faked

**Quote-worthy stat:** "Cargo theft via identity fraud increased 600% between 2021-2023"

#### 3. Document Chaos
**What it is:** Critical documents (BOL, POD, receipts) are scattered across texts, emails, faxes, and driver cab folders.

**The impact:**
- Average broker wastes 4+ hours/week hunting for documents
- Invoicing delayed because POD can't be found
- Disputes impossible to resolve without paper trail
- Drivers text docs to dispatch, dispatch forgets to forward

**Quote-worthy stat:** "38% of freight payment delays are caused by missing or incorrect documentation"

#### 4. Zero Visibility & Accountability
**What it is:** Once a load leaves pickup, brokers often have no idea where it is or who has it.

**The impact:**
- "Where's my load?" calls consume operations teams
- No way to verify driver is actually en route
- Mid-trip driver swaps happen without anyone knowing
- When loads go missing, there's no audit trail

**Quote-worthy stat:** "Average broker makes 6+ check calls per load for status updates"

#### 5. No Industry Standard
**What it is:** Unlike banking (SWIFT), payments (Stripe), or ride-sharing (Uber), freight has no unified identity or verification layer.

**The impact:**
- Every broker uses different systems
- No shared source of truth
- Carriers must re-verify with every new broker
- Fraud spreads because bad actors aren't tracked across the industry

## What Freight Passport Is

### One-Sentence Description
Freight Passport is a unified identity and verification platform that creates a single source of truth for every load — linking verified drivers, equipment, documents, and real-time location into one immutable record.

### Elevator Pitch (30 seconds)
"Every load gets a unique passport ID. Every driver must verify with photo ID and location — like Uber, but for freight. Every document uploads to one central hub visible to all parties. Every change triggers instant alerts. Every suspicious pattern gets flagged before it becomes a problem. One passport. One truth. No more blind trust."

### How It Works (Simple Version)
1. **Broker creates load** → Unique Passport ID generated
2. **Carrier accepts & assigns driver** → Driver info linked to passport
3. **Driver verifies identity** → Photo selfie + location + phone verification
4. **Load moves with full visibility** → Real-time tracking, status updates, alerts
5. **Documents uploaded in real-time** → BOL, POD visible to all parties instantly
6. **Any change triggers alerts** → All parties notified immediately
7. **Load completes with full audit trail** → Immutable record for disputes/compliance

### CATEGORY 5: Fraud Detection & Risk Scoring

#### Feature: Real-Time Fraud Scoring
- Every load has a fraud risk score (0-100)
- Score updates in real-time as events occur
- Visual indicator: Green (low), Yellow (medium), Red (high)
- Detailed breakdown of why score is elevated

#### Feature: Fraud Detection Rules

**Carrier Risk Signals:**
- New MC number (less than 6 months old)
- MC with limited inspection history
- MC with authority issues or insurance gaps
- Generic email domain (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook)
- Phone number associated with multiple MCs
- Address is residential or PO Box
- No physical presence verified

**Assignment Risk Signals:**
- Driver never verified after X hours
- Multiple driver changes before pickup
- Driver change mid-trip (HIGH RISK)
- Carrier change at any point (CRITICAL)
- Truck assigned to multiple active loads
- Driver assigned to multiple active loads

**Location Risk Signals:**
- Driver location doesn't match pickup city
- Driver location far from expected route
- No location updates for extended period
- Location jump (impossible travel time)
- Handoff locations don't match (drivers not near each other)

**Document Risk Signals:**
- No BOL uploaded X hours after pickup
- No POD uploaded X hours after delivery
- Same document image used on multiple loads
- Document data doesn't match load details
- Signature appears copied/reused

**Pattern Risk Signals:**
- Duplicate load detected in system
- Similar load booked by different broker
- Carrier has history of flagged loads
- Driver has history of verification failures

#### Feature: Duplicate Load Detection
- Search engine to find matching loads
- Automatic check when load created
- Matches on: origin, destination, date, commodity
- Alerts broker before load is booked
- "This load may already be booked elsewhere"

#### Feature: Carrier Risk Profiles
- Aggregated risk score per carrier
- Based on historical load performance
- FMCSA data integration
- Inspection and crash history
- Pattern analysis across all loads

Freight Passport's AI gets smarter with every load across the entire network. This is the key differentiator that solo tools can't match.

### How Network Learning Works
```
Fraud confirmed on Load A (Broker X)

AI analyzes all attributes of the fraud
         ↓
AI finds similar patterns across the network
         ↓
All similar carriers/patterns get increased risk scores
         ↓

AI models update to catch this pattern faster next time
```

### AI-Specific Messaging Tips:
- Always explain WHAT the AI does, not just that AI exists
- Use concrete examples: "AI reads your BOL and extracts the shipper name in 10 seconds"
- Emphasize the human benefit: "AI does the work so you don't have to"
- Connect AI to business outcomes: "AI catches fraud → you save money"
- Don't oversell: "AI helps detect fraud" not "AI eliminates all fraud"

### Tone:
- Confident, not arrogant
- Direct, not salesy
- Professional, not corporate
- Urgent (fraud is real), not fearful
- Solution-focused, not problem-dwelling
